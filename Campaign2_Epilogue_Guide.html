Campaign 2 Epilogue Guide

Epilogue Planning: A Guide for Players

As Campaign 2 draws to a close, we'll be holding an epilogue episode to honour your characters' journeys and close out their arcs. Here's how to think through what you want that to look like.

Start With the Arc, Not the Ending

Before you think about what happens, think about what your character has been about. Ask yourself:

  • What was the central wound, question, or drive that defined my character at the start?
  • How has that changed over the course of the campaign?
  • What did they gain, lose, or become that they couldn't have imagined at the beginning?

Your epilogue should feel like the answer to those questions - not necessarily a happy answer, but a true one.

Questions to Help You Shape Your Epilogue

On resolution:
  • Is there a relationship (ally, rival, loved one, enemy) that needs a final beat?
  • Is there a place your character would return to, or finally leave behind?
  • What does your character do with themselves now that it's over?
On tone:
  • Do you want your epilogue to feel triumphant, bittersweet, quiet, or something else entirely?
  • Does your character get peace - or do they carry the weight forward?
On loose ends:
  • Are there threads you'd rather leave open for a potential One Shot?
  • Are there things you don't want resolved - mysteries or tensions that feel right to leave unfinished?

Practical Things to Bring to Me

When you're ready, share:

  • A short vision - even just a sentence or two of what you imagine for your character's ending
  • Any key NPCs you want present or acknowledged
  • The emotional note you want to end on
  • Anything you'd like kept open for future play

You don't need a fully scripted scene - that's my job. Just give me the soul of it and I'll build around you.

A Few Gentle Reminders

  • Epilogues don't have to be long. Sometimes the most powerful endings are small and specific.
  • It's okay to grieve your character a little. That's part of closing something that mattered.
  • You've earned whatever ending feels right. Don't talk yourself out of the thing you actually want.

Looking forward to giving these characters the send-off they deserve. Come with your ideas - no wrong answers.
