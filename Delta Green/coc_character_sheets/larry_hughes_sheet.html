Personal Description
A seemingly meek and mild antiquity dealer and handyman of slender frame but with a surprising level of charm and attraction.
Traits
Meticulous. Careful, precise, methodical — in his work and in his thinking. Things have a right place and a right way.

Hoarder. One man's trash is another man's treasure. He keeps things. You never know when something will come in useful.
Ideology & Beliefs
1. One man's trash is another man's treasure.
2. History teaches us lessons for today that we can use for tomorrow.
3. Fix your own problems.
Phobias & Manias
Scared of birds and most winged things, such as moths. The fluttering, the unpredictability of it. He can manage in daylight but it gets to him.
Significant People
His father and grandfather who taught him all he knew — the trade, the eye for value, the patience for repair. Margaret is his best repeat customer; he met her through her late husband, who shared his passion for salvage and old things.
Meaningful Locations
Tregaw Scrap and Tip — where he rescues his pieces. There is a ritual to it. Arriving early, working methodically through the piles. Finding something worthwhile in what everyone else threw away.
Treasured Possessions
His notepad of ideas and projects — a catalogue of everything he's found, fixed, or is planning to. His tool belt, which belonged to his grandfather.
Injuries & Scars
None recorded. A working life of small cuts and calluses, but nothing worth noting.
Encounters with Strange Entities
None — outside of weird noises and shadows you see when working late at the workshop. But that's just cats and tricks of the light. Probably.
