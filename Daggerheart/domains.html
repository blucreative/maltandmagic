1. Reading Domains

Think of domains as style, pressure, and worldview

Power source

Where does this feel come from?

Arcana and Codex both touch magic, but one feels innate and raw while the other feels studied and intentional.

Scene posture

Does it push, shield, misdirect, or endure?

Valor, Bone, and Blade may all touch combat, but they approach danger through very different instincts.

Emotional texture

What does this domain feel like at the table?

Grace reads socially luminous, Midnight reads secretive, Splendor reads sacred and life-facing.

Build promise

What stories does it invite?

A good domain choice should suggest moments you are excited to play, not just stronger numbers.

Shortcut

If you are choosing between two classes, compare their domains first. The better fit is often the class whose domains describe your fantasy more accurately.

2. Domain Atlas

The nine core domains and the energy they bring

Arcana

Instinctual magic

Arcana feels like power that already lives inside the body or the world. It is intimate, volatile, and expressive rather than methodical.

  • Best for players who want magic to feel dangerous, emotional, or wild.
  • Shared by Druid and Sorcerer.
  • Usually points toward transformation, elemental force, or uncanny impulse.

Blade

Weapon mastery

Blade is martial precision. It carries confidence, technique, and clean threat, whether that shows up through swordplay, archery, or practiced violence.

  • Best for players who want control through skill instead of mysticism.
  • Shared by Guardian and Warrior.
  • Supports characters who look competent before they look flashy.

Bone

Body and tactics

Bone is discipline, endurance, and battlefield sense. It is about knowing how bodies move, how danger lands, and how to stay present when pressure spikes.

  • Best for players who like tactical groundedness and physical credibility.
  • Shared by Ranger and Warrior.
  • Often reads relentless, trained, or predatory depending on the class around it.

Codex

Learned magic

Codex is studied magical knowledge: books, sigils, formal learning, memory, and clever structure. It turns expertise into power.

  • Best for players who like prepared tools and thoughtful spell use.
  • Shared by Bard and Wizard.
  • Feels elegant, literate, and deliberate in fiction.

Grace

Charisma and perception

Grace is about shaping what people notice, believe, and follow. It can be charming, glamorous, deceptive, or piercingly intuitive.

  • Best for players who want their personality to change scenes.
  • Shared by Bard and Rogue.
  • Creates characters who are socially dangerous in the best possible way.

Midnight

Shadows and secrecy

Midnight governs hidden truths, stealth, obscurity, and misdirection. It is the domain of things glimpsed out of the corner of the eye.

  • Best for players who enjoy secrets, vanishing acts, and sharp reveals.
  • Shared by Rogue and Sorcerer.
  • Can read as either subtle menace or mystical mystery depending on context.

Sage

Nature and vitality

Sage is living-world wisdom, vitality, ferocity, and rooted instinct. It points toward growth, the hunt, and nature as active force.

  • Best for players who want wilderness identity or primal attunement.
  • Shared by Druid and Ranger.
  • Often supports exploration, survival, and bestial momentum.

Splendor

Life and death

Splendor carries healing, radiance, restoration, and direct contact with life’s fragile edge. It often feels sacred, luminous, and consequential.

  • Best for players who want miracles, rescue, and life-facing drama.
  • Shared by Seraph and Wizard.
  • Can feel noble, priestly, or mystically reverent without losing bite.

Valor

Protection and force

Valor is courage made practical. It is about keeping danger off others, bracing under pressure, and making steadfastness feel active.

  • Best for players who want duty, defense, and visible backbone.
  • Shared by Guardian and Seraph.
  • Frequently defines the emotional center of a party.

3. Class Pairings

Every class through the lens of its two domains

Bard

Grace + Codex

Social brilliance plus learned structure. The Bard feels like a performer, rhetorician, or manipulator whose art has technique behind it.

Druid

Sage + Arcana

Nature’s instinct fused with wild magic. The Druid feels alive, changeable, and closely bonded to place or transformation.

Guardian

Valor + Blade

Protective resolve expressed through trained weaponcraft. The Guardian protects because they know exactly how to stand and strike.

Ranger

Bone + Sage

Physical discipline plus living-world instinct. The Ranger feels like a hunter who reads danger with both body knowledge and natural attunement.

Rogue

Midnight + Grace

Secrets sharpened by social finesse. The Rogue disappears, deceives, reads the room, and exploits fractures with style.

Seraph

Splendor + Valor

Radiant compassion backed by courage. The Seraph feels sacred, brave, and strongly motivated by who deserves saving.

Sorcerer

Arcana + Midnight

Innate magic mixed with mystery and hidden force. The Sorcerer feels volatile, magnetic, and slightly dangerous even when helping.

Warrior

Blade + Bone

Technique paired with discipline. The Warrior is the clearest expression of martial mastery, grounded, consistent, and hard to shake.

Wizard

Codex + Splendor

Studied power meeting luminous life-facing magic. The Wizard feels like a scholar whose expertise can heal, protect, or solve elegantly.

4. Build Sparks

Quick inspiration if you are still deciding

For direct heroism

Valor or Splendor

Start here if you want your character to radiate courage, guardianship, healing, or open-hearted conviction.

For sharp cleverness

Grace or Midnight

Pick one of these if you want to outmaneuver scenes through charm, deception, stealth, or social intelligence.

For force of nature energy

Arcana or Sage

These domains suit players who want primal, instinctive, or wild-feeling scenes more than refined ceremony.
