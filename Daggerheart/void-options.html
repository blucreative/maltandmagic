1. How to Use

Treat the Void material as an explicit campaign choice, not hidden bonus content

If you want more occult texture

Open Witch early

Witch belongs on the same decision table as the core classes when your group wants charms, hexes, strange bargains, and communion-heavy play.

If you want sharper lethality

Open Assassin intentionally

Assassin fits best when stealth, marked targets, extraction pressure, and violent precision suit the campaign rather than distorting it.

If you want more identity variance

Use Heritage and Transformations as live tools

These options widen ancestry support and late-stage identity shifts, making them character-defining rather than optional trivia.

2. Void Classes

Witch and Assassin belong in the class conversation when the table allows Void content

Witch

The Void class

The Witch is built for players who want occult atmosphere, charms, hexes, communion, and the feeling that power comes with intimacy and risk.

  • Best for players who want personal magic to feel ritual, uncanny, and relational.
  • Strong fit for darker, stranger, or more folkloric campaign tones.
  • Often appeals to players who are deciding between Bard, Druid, and Sorcerer but want something more haunted or covenant-shaped.

Assassin

The Void class

The Assassin focuses on stealth, marked targets, extraction, and lethal precision. It is the option for players who want sharper mission pressure than a generalist Rogue implies.

  • Best for players who want hit-and-fade pressure, pursuit payoff, and target-focused scenes.
  • Strong fit for intrigue, covert operations, vendettas, and campaigns where timing matters.
  • Often appeals to players choosing between Rogue and Ranger who want a more explicitly lethal identity.
Practical build advice

If Void classes are open, players should compare Witch against the game’s other occult or primal casters, and Assassin against the stealth-forward core options, before locking a concept. That keeps the choice grounded in scene feel instead of novelty alone.

3. Supplemental Options

Heritage and Transformations widen who a character can become

Supplemental ancestry support

Heritage

Heritage options expand the ancestry and community space available to players. They matter most when your table wants a wider menu of lineage identity than the base core assumptions provide.

Narrative identity shift

Transformations

Transformations are high-impact upgrades that come with both upside and cost. They are best treated as meaningful identity events rather than casual bonus pick-ups.

When to surface these

Bring Heritage into session zero when lineage breadth matters up front. Bring Transformations into campaign planning when the table is interested in irreversible or identity-shifting developments during play.

4. Table Guidance

Decide how open the Void menu is before character creation starts

Open by default

Use all options as live player content

Best for tables already committed to wider tonal range and more expansive build diversity.

Selective open

Approve by campaign tone

Best when Witch, Assassin, or Transformations fit some campaign frames strongly but feel out of place in others.

Delayed unlock

Reserve some material for discovery

Best when Transformations or certain Void-flavored heritage pieces are meant to arrive through play rather than at character creation.
