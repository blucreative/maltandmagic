1. How to Choose

Choose the class that gives you the right kind of spotlight

If you want to lead danger

Play someone who can absorb pressure

Guardian and Seraph naturally step into scenes where the party needs a shield, a vow, or a rescue button.

If you want control

Play someone who changes the rhythm

Bard, Rogue, Wizard, Ranger, and even some Void options all influence who gets the next clean angle, just through very different flavors.

If you want intensity

Play someone who escalates the fiction

Warrior, Sorcerer, and Druid thrive when the scene needs force, volatility, or a decisive swing.

Best heuristic

Ask which sentence feels best: “I save people,” “I make openings,” “I hit like a storm,” or “I solve problems with magic.” That answer usually narrows the field immediately.

2. Class Atlas

The core lineup plus Witch and Assassin at player speed

Bard

Grace + Codex

The Bard bends scenes with presence, wit, artistry, and social gravity. It is the class for players who want their words, timing, and confidence to matter as much as any blade.

Evasion 10 HP 5 Role: support controller
  • Best at giving allies openings and making enemies hesitate.
  • Strong if you love social play, momentum swings, and stylish saves.
  • Feels like a spotlight conductor rather than a passive backline caster.

Druid

Sage + Arcana

The Druid channels primal force, elemental transformation, and living-world authority. It rewards players who want adaptability and a character identity tied tightly to place, instinct, and change.

Evasion 10 HP 6 Role: adaptive caster
  • Excellent when you want to flex between ferocity, resilience, and utility.
  • Works for players who like wild imagery and fiction-rich shapeshifting energy.
  • Brings primal texture to travel, survival, and magical conflict scenes.

Guardian

Valor + Blade

The Guardian is the classic wall between danger and the people who matter. It is for players who enjoy taking the hit, dictating enemy attention, and making the team feel harder to break.

Evasion 9 HP 7 Role: protector
  • Best when the table needs a clear defender with moral and physical weight.
  • Turns survival into leadership rather than passive tanking.
  • Pairs well with fragile high-impact allies who need room to operate.

Ranger

Bone + Sage

The Ranger thrives on pursuit, angle control, wilderness instinct, and patient pressure. It suits players who want to read the scene like a hunt and strike where it matters most.

Evasion 12 HP 6 Role: hunter-controller
  • Great for mobility, tracking, focus-fire, and companion-forward fiction.
  • Shines in campaigns with travel, danger signs, and layered terrain.
  • Feels methodical rather than flashy, but can dominate through positioning.

Rogue

Midnight + Grace

The Rogue is all about leverage: being where nobody expected, knowing what nobody told you, and cashing in on the split-second weakness everyone else missed.

Evasion 12 HP 6 Role: infiltrator
  • Ideal for players who like stealth, improvisation, and tactical opportunism.
  • Brings immediate value in social infiltration as well as combat spikes.
  • Often feels like the party’s scalpel, scout, and troublemaker at once.

Seraph

Splendor + Valor

The Seraph combines sacred presence, battlefield courage, healing, and miracle energy. It fits players who want to protect others without giving up dramatic authority or divine weight.

Evasion 9 HP 7 Role: holy bulwark
  • Excellent for rescue play, blessings, and emotional anchor moments.
  • Can feel like a paladin, battle-priest, or radiant guardian depending on fiction.
  • Especially strong in parties that want courage and healing in one seat.

Sorcerer

Arcana + Midnight

The Sorcerer wields power that already lives inside them. It is volatile, intimate, and dramatic, perfect for players who want magic to feel dangerous, emotional, and a little impossible to ignore.

Evasion 10 HP 6 Role: arcane striker
  • Great for explosive moments, identity conflict, and magical swagger.
  • Turns inner power into a roleplaying engine, not just a spell list.
  • Works best when you want to push scenes hotter rather than safer.

Warrior

Blade + Bone

The Warrior is the most direct expression of martial mastery: trained, dangerous, and reliable under pressure. It is for players who want a weapon-forward identity with room for personal style.

Evasion 11 HP 6 Role: weapons expert
  • Consistent, sturdy, and satisfying if you want action economy to feel clean.
  • Lets your tactics come from judgment and positioning rather than spell setup.
  • Often the easiest recommendation for a new player who wants to hit hard and stay relevant.

Wizard

Codex + Splendor

The Wizard is deliberate magical expertise: learned power, elegant solutions, and a toolkit that rewards foresight. It is ideal for players who enjoy planning, versatility, and resourceful spell use.

Evasion 11 HP 5 Role: scholar-caster
  • Strong when you want utility, breadth, and clever answers.
  • Feels less reckless than Sorcerer and more prepared for complex scenes.
  • Often the best pick for players who love magical problem-solving.

Witch

The Void option

The Witch is for players who want charms, hexes, communion, and occult atmosphere to sit at the center of their identity. Its power feels intimate, negotiated, and uncanny rather than merely explosive.

Role: occult caster Track tone with your table Use when Void is open
  • Best for players who want ritual, bargains, and haunted magical texture.
  • Strong fit for stranger, darker, or more folkloric campaigns.
  • Compare against Druid and Sorcerer if you want to test how occult your magic should feel.

Assassin

The Void option

The Assassin is a sharper lethal specialist than the broader Rogue fantasy. It is built for target pressure, stealth-driven missions, extraction, and the feeling that every scene may hinge on a single decisive strike.

Role: lethal infiltrator Track tone with your table Use when Void is open
  • Best for players who want mission-shaped stealth and narrower, deadlier intent.
  • Strong fit for intrigue, vendetta, covert action, and campaigns where timing matters.
  • Compare against Rogue and Ranger if you want to test how specialized your precision play should be.
Void options are live choices, not hidden extras

If your table allows the Void material, Witch and Assassin should sit on the same decision shelf as the core classes. Use the dedicated Void reference for Heritage support and Transformations when you want the broader optional menu live during character creation.

3. Party Chemistry

How classes tend to make each other shine

Protection + impact

Guardian or Seraph plus a swing class

Put a defender beside Sorcerer, Bard, or Wizard when you want the party to feel brave enough to attempt difficult, flashy plays.

Setup + execution

Bard or Rogue with Warrior or Ranger

One class creates the angle, the other cashes it in. This combo is excellent if your group enjoys sharp teamwork and scene handoffs.

Wild card + stabilizer

Druid or Sorcerer with Seraph or Bard

Volatile or primal energy feels even better when another character can redirect momentum, repair damage, or calm the emotional weather.

No dead roles

Daggerheart is built so classes overlap in useful ways. You do not need a mandatory healer or a mandatory tank. What matters more is whether your party can create openings, survive pressure, and contribute to the fiction in distinct voices.

4. Quick Picks

Use these if you want a recommendation in under thirty seconds

Easiest starting point

Warrior or Guardian

Choose Warrior if you want direct offense. Choose Guardian if you want defense to feel active and important.

Most social momentum

Bard

The Bard is a superb pick if you want to affect both conversations and combat without feeling locked into one mode.

Most magical intensity

Sorcerer or Wizard

Take Sorcerer for dramatic inner power. Take Wizard for deliberate expertise and a deeper sense of preparation.

Most occult atmosphere

Witch

Take Witch if you want strange bargains, hexes, and power that feels intimate, ritualized, and haunted.
