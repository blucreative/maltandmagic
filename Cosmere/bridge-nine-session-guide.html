II.

World Primer — Welcome to Roshar

A fast briefing for anyone in the room (or watching at home) who hasn't read the novels. Everything here is common knowledge to a citizen of eastern Roshar — no spoilers.

Highstorms & Stormlight

Hurricane-force storms roll across Roshar from east to west roughly once every five days. They're violent enough to hurl boulders — but they also infuse the world with Stormlight, the energy behind magic, Shardplate, and fabrial devices.

Spheres

Gemstones sealed in glass are Roshar's universal currency. Infused with Stormlight they glow for days, doubling as a light source — merchants are wary of "dun" spheres that have run dark.

Spren

Spirit-like manifestations that react to emotion and nature: lifespren hover over healthy plants, angerspren pool like blood at a soldier's feet, windspren chase anything moving fast. Mostly background texture — except for the rare, sapient Radiant spren who seek out worthy people to bond.

Shardbearers

Shardblades cut through anything non-living and kill with a single strike. Shardplate deflects a Blade and grants superhuman strength and speed. A single Shardbearer can turn a battle — nations go to war over one set.

The Vorin Church

Eastern Roshar's dominant religion. Ardent priests serve commoners and nobles alike. Vorinism enforces a rigid caste system — lighteyed nobility over darkeyed commoners — and reversed gender roles: women read, scheme, and scholar; men fight. Slavery is legal and common.

The Alethi

Bridge Nine's setting. A militaristic, honor-obsessed kingdom recently unified under House Kholin. Highprinces still jockey for status through duels and border skirmishes. Soldiers sometimes succumb to the Thrill — a battle-fury that strips away restraint.

The Listeners

Called "Parshendi" by the Alethi, these red-black-white marbled people are sapient cousins of the enslaved parshmen. They fight in singing warpairs, field their own Shardbearers, and fiercely defend the Shattered Plains as their home.

The Shattered Plains

A vast expanse of wind-carved chasms and plateaus on Alethkar's eastern edge. Chasmfiends — armored apex predators — roam the depths guarding gemhearts worth a fortune. It's the site of an uneasy, ongoing war between the Alethi and the listeners — and exactly where tonight's story begins.

III.

The Core Mechanic

Every skill test in the Cosmere RPG works the same way.

d20 + attribute + skill ranks

Difficulty

  • DC 10 — an average test
  • DC 15 — a challenging test
  • Beat the DC, you succeed. Simple as that.

Raising the Stakes

  • On risky, dramatic, or plot-critical tests, add the plot die to the roll.
  • It can swing a result toward triumph or disaster — that's the point.

V.

The Plot Die

A standard d6 doubles as the plot die. Use the widget in the sidebar at the table, or map results by hand:

RollResult
1 – 2Complication (two faces)
3 – 4Blank — no effect
5 – 6Opportunity (two faces)

Spending an Opportunity (or a natural 20)

  • Aid an Ally — their next test gains advantage
  • Collect Yourself — recover 1 focus
  • Critically Hit — turn a hit into a critical hit
  • Influence the Narrative — a positive plot twist

Facing a Complication (or a natural 1)

  • Hinder an Ally — the next PC test gains disadvantage
  • Become Distracted — lose 1 focus
  • Influence the Narrative — a negative plot twist
  • GM's call — keep it cinematic, not punitive

Plot Twists — Combat

  • Opportunity: friendly reinforcements arrive; a wounded enemy flees; a bystander escapes; an enemy drops their weapon; a spren manifests and helps you.
  • Complication: enemy reinforcements arrive; an NPC is knocked out; an ally is endangered; you drop or damage your gear; a spren manifests and distracts you.

Plot Twists — Conversation

  • Opportunity: you recall a helpful fact; an observer is impressed; a spren reveals something unexpected; you cover your real intentions.
  • Complication: something you "knew" turns out false; an observer is scornful; a spren embarrasses you; you accidentally reveal a secret.

IX.

Episode Guide

Two two-hour episodes, recorded in one night. Episode 1 is survival and action; Episode 2 turns to mystery and exploration. cut marks beats to trim or compress if the clock is tight.

Episode One — "Bridge Nine"

~2 Hours · Action / Survival

A routine expedition ends in a fall, a betrayal, and a chase through the dark.

  1. Prologue & roles. Read the opening, assign expedition roles. Keep to 5–10 minutes — this is table setup, not a scene.
  2. The Journey. Cross the bridge; the chull spooks. Deduction/Survival or Leadership tests set up advantage for what's coming.
  3. The Attack. The chasmfiend surfaces, the bridge collapses. Raise the stakes on every Agility/Athletics test. This is your cold open — let it be loud.
  4. Survivors & First Impressions. Establish who's left: Selinar, Varda, Matal, Doral, Aran, Khesh. One physical detail per PC.
  5. Picking up the Pieces. Search for survivors, food, water, tools. Offer 2 of the 6 options rather than the full menu. trim
  6. Fight! Selinar succumbs to the Thrill and turns on Doral. Combat or a tense Persuasion/Leadership stand-down.
  7. Chasmfiend Pursuit → Taking Flight. The chull is eaten; the beast returns. Run the escape endeavor — 6 successes before 4 failures. Your centerpiece set piece — narrate every roll.
  8. Safe for Now. The party squeezes through the crevice. End the episode here — hard cut before Matal opens her map.

Episode Two — "The Lost Shrine"

~2 Hours · Mystery / Exploration

A night's rest, a forgotten shrine, and a choice about how the story ends.

  1. Cold open recap. Thirty seconds on what happened, then straight into A Night's Rest — see Campfire Prompts below.
  2. To the Ruins. Matal's map points northeast. Pass the three bodies in the water — a quiet, eerie beat before the dungeon.
  3. The Lost Shrine — Ground Floor. Climb the rubble, find a light source, enter the dark.
  4. Stairway. Cremling nests, Agility tests, possible swarm. Resolve the swarm in one fast exchange rather than a full 3-round grind. trim
  5. Top Floor — The Glyphs. The Willshaper statue and the Soulcaster puzzle. Run the endeavor as a quick narrated montage rather than test-by-test if energy's flagging. optional trim
  6. The Surface. Exit north (surprised) or south (safe) — singers are waiting either way.
  7. Encountering the Singers. Fight, or talk Vexla down. Let the table's tone from Episode 1 decide which way this leans.
  8. The Way Home. Twelve hours to the warcamps, then the final plateau encounter — or a peaceful arrival.
  9. Epilogue. Resolve the Soulcaster's fate, advance everyone to 2nd level, and land the ending. Since this is standalone, tie it off clean — no dangling threads.
X.

Campfire Prompts

Before the party sleeps, give each player one of these — answered in a sentence or two, in character or out. Don't dig deeper than that; the goal is texture, not a backstory episode.

  • "Which of you has committed a crime? What was the crime?"
  • "Who among you betrayed or failed a loved one? What happened to them?"
  • "Who here has been wrongly condemned? Why were you falsely accused?"
  • "Which of you has survived another tragedy like this? What were the circumstances?"
  • "Who here is in love? Who is your loved one, and are the feelings mutual?"
  • "Which of you has an enemy? Why have they sworn to kill you?"

Spren Watch — quiet GM tracking

  • Tone (lightspren) favors characters who seek freedom — fighting unjust treatment, freeing the bound, mercy toward listeners.
  • Aeliran (honorspren) favors characters who embody protection — shielding the weak, risking harm for someone vulnerable.
  • Mark privately through both episodes. Whoever's marked most by the epilogue may take the First Ideal (Windrunner or Willshaper) going to 2nd level.
